Human reviewers devote roughly a third of their feedback to code style and consistency, often tying those comments to the bigger picture of project architecture. They also dive into testing, CI/CD processes, and documentation concerns far more than bots do. AI reviewers, on the other hand, spend the bulk of their comments on style rules and policy enforcement, rarely venturing into design discussions.

3. Tone & Language

  • Human reviewers soften feedback and frequently include context or rationale. Comments often start with polite words like “please” or qualifiers such as “nit:”. One maintainer explained: “Please don’t set any kind of ‘auto-fix’. CI’s role is to check everything is OK… not to replace the contributor doing things right.” (CI philosophy guidance) This tone of mentorship and reasoning is a hallmark of human reviews.
  • AI reviewers are blunt, factual, and impersonal—no greetings or pleasantries. They will plainly state issues such as “unused variable detected” without explanation. (Lint bot unused-var check) While efficient, this style lacks the nuance and back-and-forth that humans provide.
